# Computing in the Philippines: Local Technology History

This exhibit delves into the history and evolution of computing technology in the Philippines, tracing its journey from its early beginnings to the present day. Through an interactive timeline and map, visitors will discover the key milestones, organizations, and technological advancements that contributed to the growth of the Philippine technology sector.

The exhibit will highlight how computing evolved from a limited resource used by government agencies, universities, and large businesses into a nationwide success with the country's software development, internet services, and other emerging technologies.

### Exhibit Roadmap

* **The Early Years of Computing (1960s–1980s)**
* **The Birth of the Philippine Internet (1990s)**

  * PHNET and the country's first internet connection
* **The Expansion of Technology Across the Country (2000s)**

  * Growth of internet access and digital infrastructure
* **The Rise of the IT Industry**
* **Local Innovations and Digital Services**

  * Filipino technology companies, startups, and digital services
* **Role of Universities, Research Institutions, and Government Agencies**

## Interactive Elements

### 1. Philippine Computing History Map

An interactive timeline and map that allows visitors to explore significant milestones in the history of computing and technology in the Philippines.

Visitors can drag a year slider spanning from the 1960s to the present day. As the timeline progresses, the map updates to reveal important locations, organizations, and technological developments that shaped the country's computing landscape.

**Key Features:**

* Interactive year slider
* Dynamic map visualization
* Clickable hotspots
* Historical photographs and technological artifacts
* Local innovation highlights

Selecting a location reveals:

* Historical background
* Associated organizations
* Major technological contributions
* Images and supporting materials
* Relevant milestones from the selected period

*This interactive element provides historical context while allowing visitors to visualize how computing technology expanded throughout the Philippines over time.*

---

### 2. Philippine Computing Knowledge Challenge

A gamified quiz interface that tests visitors on the milestones, locations, organizations, and historical breakthroughs showcased throughout the exhibit.

The quiz serves as a reinforcement activity that encourages visitors to engage with and retain information presented in the interactive map.

**Quiz Features:**

* Multiple-choice questions
* True-or-false questions
* Questions based on map locations and historical events
* Coverage of multiple eras of Philippine computing history
* Immediate feedback and explanations
* Final score summary
* Technology-themed achievement badges

Sample topics include:

* Early computing systems in the Philippines
* PHNET and the first internet connection
* Growth of internet access
* Philippine IT industry milestones
* Universities and government agencies involved in technology development

*This interactive element promotes active learning and allows visitors to assess their understanding of Philippine computing history.*

# Incremental Development Log (Checkpoint 1)

## Development Progress

During this development phase, the group focused on building the foundation of the virtual exhibit. Rather than implementing every planned feature immediately, we concentrated on creating the core structure that future interactive elements will build upon.

### Completed Features

- Set up the Astro project, Leaflet, and project structure.
- Integrated React components into the Astro application.
- Created the initial interactive timeline layout.
- Implemented a working Leaflet map displaying the Philippines.
- Added a basic information panel that updates based on the selected timeline event.
- Developed the initial version of the Philippine Computing Knowledge Challenge quiz interface.
- Organized project files into reusable components to simplify future development.

Although these features are still in their early stages, they establish the overall framework of the exhibit and provide a working prototype for future improvements.

---

## Aha Moments / Things Learned

One of the biggest realizations during development was discovering that some required dependencies had not been installed. Initially, this prevented some components—particularly the interactive map—from functioning correctly. After installing the necessary packages and configuring the project properly, we were able to successfully integrate the Leaflet map into the website.

Throughout the development process, we also gained a better understanding of how Astro and React work together. Since most of the members aren't familiar using these things, we learned alot along the way. Astro handles the page structure while React powers the interactive components such as the timeline and future map interactions. This separation helped us organize the project into modular and reusable components.

---

## Challenges Encountered

Some of the challenges we encountered include:

- Setting up some of the development environment and required dependencies.
- Integrating React components inside an Astro project.
- Configuring the Leaflet map correctly.
- Planning the overall architecture while keeping the components modular for future expansion.

Most of these challenges were resolved through testing, debugging, and gradually building each feature one component at a time.
